Geoff Kimball

Library of Embarrassment

The earliest I can remember dabbling in web design is at the age of 15 or so. Recently, I found the hard drive from the computer I used all through high school, and against my better judgement, I copied all the files that I could find and took a horrifying trip down memory lane. There's homework, study guides, lyrics for songs, a ton of edgy graphic design, and about two dozen websites in various states of completion.

The websites all have one common theme, which is video games. Turns out that I was a gigantic nerd in high school, which remains true to this day. Most of the designs are for speculative game review sites that I wanted to make. I spent most of my web browsing time in high school on Gamespot, so most of my reference material when learning to build websites was video game sites.

There's a few other choice things buried in this archive. You can find a few sketches of personal websites. None of them have any content beyond an index page, but you can read the navigation of each site to get an idea of what I was going for. One design has a nav containing "my blog", "game reviews", "game maker games", "digital art", "wallpaper", "contact", and "external links". No 16-year-old truly knows who they are as a person, but apparently I had a vague idea of who I wanted to be.

Below are every single one of these websites, in their unaltered form. Most of them don't have any functioning links, and some barely have any content or even layout. As a collection, they present a view into my interests at the time, and my horrible taste in graphic design.
